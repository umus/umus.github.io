UT MAT157: First Session

07 Oct 2016

Team,

I am grateful to all of you who has come today and made the session productive despite some element of chaos which is often present in introductory meetings. And thank you for all the suggestions – they would definitely help us make our time together worthwhile.

One of the questions raised in the session was about the combination of training for MAT157 and MAT240 in one session. Everyone is free to choose any topic or problem to go through during the session, but I do think it is necessary to have the topic of the main discussion in mind before each session. My suggestion is to split officially the current time period in half, and devote one half to analysis and one half to algebra.

This would allow us to have a clear focus for a set period of time. As Fiona proposed, it would be great to devote some time in the beginning of each corresponding session to a quick recap of the week lectures, since this will give us a headstart with problems, hopefully identify the gaps in our knowledge and understanding as well as unite our efforts in a coherent discussion interesting for us all. If you have the outlines of lectures in any format or want to write them, please write me an email.

Alan expressed interest in reserving B026 as our base for the future sessions. I will write to ACE as soon as possible.

Matthew also suggested to create a Facebook group to spread information. If there is a demand and there are volunteers to look after the page, I am happy if you get involved with it, but would like to keep the logs of the sessions, announcements and resources on the umu website. For those of you who are new, please be aware there is a Facebook chat in which most of the instantaneous communication takes place.

If you want anything to be posted on the ut.umu page, please feel free to contact me.

Unfortunately, not every person who has participated today has written me an email. Registering is important if you want to obtain CCR after attending at least 4 sessions and having 1 academic experience.

The academic experience is supposed to add to our knowledge and academic skills development, and may involve various activities. We may book a mathematical (or any other, if there is a demand) research consultation, visit office hours of the beloved Joe Repka and Eckhard Meinrenken, a tutoring centre or a TA, or we may attend a workshop in the library. Please write what you think suits you best by email or in the Facebook chat.

If you had not already emailed me but attended the session, I would be grateful if you could fill the following form to register the attendance and send it by email from your mail.utoronto.ca address:

  1. First Name
  2. Are there any specific topics you want to go through in the next session, other than the content of the most recent lectures?
  3. What rules would you like the group to follow?
  4. Do you want your name to appear publicly in the register? If not, you may choose a nickname to be used instead. Currently, almost all names are listed in full. If you want to replace your name with something else, please drop me an email.

I am looking forward to hearing from you.

Onwards!

— sasha
